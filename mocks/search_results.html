Dan Riley, "Generation Atheist"
We will be hosting author Dan Riley for a lecture and discussion about his book, Generation Atheist. From 2008-2011, Dan Riley worked as a campus organizer in the outreach department at the non-profit think tank the Center for Inquiry. He got to know many secular student leaders during his time with CFI. Finding many of their personal journeys to atheism to be fascinating, compelling, and unique, he decided to create a book that tells their stories. Free and open to all. Hosted by the Atheists, Humanists, and Agnostics (AHA!) @ Stanford. Sponsored by Stanford Graduate Student Council and the ASSU Undergraduate Senate.

Galette des Rois (King's cake) organized by the French Stanford Student Association
Have you ever wanted to be a king for a day? Or just to taste an almond filled French pastry? Then, come draw the kings and taste a "galette des rois" with the French Stanford Student Association!!! The King's cake tradition celebrates Epiphany beginning of January in France. The King's cake is a circular cake made of flaky puff pastry layers with a dense center of frangipane (almond filling). A figurine is hidden in the cake and the person who finds it in their slice becomes "king" and wins a crown! For the occasion, the French Stanford Student Association will buy French King's cakes and will serve them with quiches, salads and cider. When: Wednesday, January 15th, 2014, from 6:30pm to 9pm Where: the Assembly Room at the Bechtel International Center This event is free and open to all. Bring your friends! Please bring your ID if you want to drink wine or hard cider. This event is sponsored by the Graduate Student Council, the Billie Achilles fund and the Bechtel International Center.

Symposium: Arts and Music from Tibet
This symposium will feature musicians and scholars from Tibet. Professor Jiayong Qunpei of China's Minzu (Minority Nationalities) University will discuss music from Tibet, especially the Tibetan opera tradition. Reshi Tsering Tan, a popular and well-respected Tibetan recording artist, will share his experience as founder of the Shangri-La Folk Music Preservation Association (SFMPA). SFMPA is a non-profit organization devoted to making field recordings of traditional Tibetan music, especially fast-vanishing religious music. Select Tibetan musicians from the Tibetan Opera and Music Troupe of Qinghai Regong will demonstrate various types of Tibetan traditional and monastic music.
